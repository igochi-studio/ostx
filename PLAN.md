# ostx — Life Operating System

> Pronounced "austics", derived from caustics. A play on "OS" — an operating system for your life.

## The Soul

Caustics are the light patterns created when light refracts through water and lands on a surface below. Your life is the water — moving, shifting, alive. ostx is the sandy floor where the light lands, making the chaos legible, beautiful, and yours.

This is not a productivity app. This is a **presence** app. It lives with you, in your timespace.

## The Four Shapes

Not categories. Not tabs. **Shapes** — pre-verbal, understood before they're read.

### 1. Pool — The Concave ∪

An open vessel. A cupped palm. A bowl filling up.

**What lives here:** Anything that needs you *now*. Overdue tasks, unresolved tensions, things weighing on you, that email you're avoiding, the conversation you need to have. Not just "urgent tasks" — anything that's **sitting in you uncomfortably**.

**Why the shape works:** A concave fills up. When there's a lot in your Pool, the shape is visibly fuller — the water line rises. When you resolve things, the level drops. You can *see* your emotional load as a liquid level inside a vessel.

- Empty pool = peace
- Full pool = something needs to give

**The subtle trick:** Items don't just get added to Pool manually. Things *flow into* Pool from the other three shapes when they've been ignored too long or when you've marked something as heavy. The pool fills itself if you're not attending to your life. That's not punishment — that's honesty.

### 2. Pebble — The Rounded Rectangle ◯̲

A smooth stone. A thing you can hold. Solid but soft.

**What lives here:** The day-to-day. Tasks, to-dos, errands, projects. The things you're *carrying*. Not urgent, not emotional — just the weight of being a person with things to do.

**Why the shape works:** Pebbles are small, holdable, finite. Each task is a pebble. You can see how many you're carrying. The shape is grounded, settled — it sits at the bottom. You pick pebbles up, you put them down.

**The flow:** A pebble that sits too long without being touched starts to drift toward the Pool. The app doesn't scold — the pebble just... migrates. Because that's what neglected things do.

### 3. Droplet — The Teardrop ◧

A single drop of water. Falling or rising — ambiguous. A thought arriving or a feeling leaving.

**What lives here:** Your inner world. Thoughts, reflections, feelings, ideas, things you noticed, gratitude, frustrations, dreams. The unstructured, unformatted stream of being alive.

**Why the shape works:** A droplet is the smallest unit of water. Each thought is one drop. They accumulate into something — a puddle, a stream, an ocean — but individually they're just moments of noticing. The teardrop shape is emotional without being melodramatic.

**The flow:** Droplets that recur (same theme, same feeling, same person's name appearing) gently cluster. The app notices patterns in your drops before you do and surfaces them as gentle observations.

### 4. Ring — The Circle ◯

A closed curve. No beginning, no end. A hug seen from above. An orbit.

**What lives here:** Your people. Relationships, connections, the humans in your orbit. Not contacts — the people who take up emotional space in your life.

**Why the shape works:** A ring is a bond. It's also an orbit — people at different distances from your center. Close friends are tight rings. Acquaintances are wide orbits. The shape naturally encodes intimacy as distance. And a ring has no sharp edges.

**The flow:** When you haven't mentioned or interacted with someone in a while, their ring slowly expands — they're drifting to a wider orbit. Not gone. Just further. The app doesn't say "you should call them." Their ring just gets a little wider, and you notice.

## The Water Cycle — How Shapes Flow Into Each Other

The four shapes aren't silos. They're a living water cycle:

```
    Droplet (inner world)
        ↓ thoughts become tasks
    Pebble (daily carrying)
        ↓ neglected things become weight
    Pool (needs attention)
        ↑ resolved things become reflections
    Droplet ←───────────────┘

    Ring (people) ←→ touches all three
    (people create thoughts, tasks, and urgencies)
```

- A **Droplet** (thought) can crystallize into a **Pebble** (task) when you realize it needs action
- A **Pebble** ignored too long migrates into the **Pool** (it's now bothering you)
- When you resolve something from the **Pool**, it can leave a **Droplet** behind (a reflection)
- **Ring** entries (people) generate items in all three — a person can spark a thought, a to-do, or an urgency

## Waves — Time as Water

Time in ostx doesn't move left-to-right on a line. It **rolls in and pulls back**, like waves on a shore. Because that's how time actually feels.

### The Three Waves

#### Ripple — Now / Today

The smallest, fastest wave. Always present — a gentle pulse on the surface. Your current moment.

- The Ripple IS the caustic light pattern on the home surface — always moving, always present
- Today's items sit *on* the Ripple. They move with it. They're alive in the current moment
- The Ripple resets at **dawn** (actual sunrise at your location), not midnight — a new day starts when light returns, not when a number changes

#### Swell — This Week / Recent

The medium wave. Slower, deeper. It rises and falls across your week.

- Scroll down from the home surface and the Ripple gives way to the Swell
- Each wave crest is a day
- Wave height reflects how much was in your Pool that day — tall swells = heavy days, shallow = light
- You can see your week's emotional topology as a waveform
- Yesterday's unfinished Pebbles are in the nearest wave behind you
- Last Tuesday's are further back — smaller, softer, less defined

#### Tide — The Long Arc / Months & Seasons

The largest, slowest wave. You don't see it move — you only notice it's shifted over time.

- High tide (busy period) — water level higher on screen, caustics denser, more active
- Low tide (calm period) — more "sand" exposed, caustics sparse and slow, more breathing room
- Calculated from activity density across weeks — a mirror, not a setting
- Shifts with actual seasons — cooler blue-pearl in winter, warmer blush-gold in summer
- The app breathes with the year

### How Waves Interact with Shapes

**Pool + Ripple:** Pool fill level is based on today's Ripple. At dawn, anything settled yesterday recedes into the Swell. What remains carried over.

**Pebbles + Swell:** Untouched Pebbles drift backward into the Swell. They get waterlogged — slightly darker, slightly deeper. Eventually they migrate to the Pool.

**Droplets + Tide:** Thoughts accumulate over the long arc. The Tide view shows your inner world across months as a depth map. Dense Droplet clusters = deep water. Sparse periods = shallow.

**Rings + All Three:** A Ring in the Ripple = interacted today. In the Swell = it's been days. Only in the Tide = weeks or months — their orbit has widened.

### The Scroll = Diving Deeper in Time

```
Surface (now)          — Ripple — caustic light, today's items
    ↓ scroll down
Shallow water (recent) — Swell — this week, recent waves
    ↓ scroll deeper
Deep water (past)      — Tide — months, seasons, the long view
```

As you scroll down, the visual environment shifts:

- **Surface**: bright, warm pearl light, active caustics, clear visibility
- **Shallow**: slightly deeper blue, caustics softer, light filtering down
- **Deep**: muted, quiet, light is distant above, things are still and preserved

### Dawn — Slack Water

At actual sunrise, there's a brief moment — **slack water** — where the Ripple is perfectly still. Nothing from yesterday has carried over yet. The surface is clean. For a few seconds, the app is just light on sand. Then the new day's items gently surface.

That moment is the most beautiful thing in the app.

### Wave Memory

Over time, wave history becomes a landscape. Pinch to zoom out on Swell/Tide:

- Stormy periods are jagged, dense
- Calm periods are smooth, low
- You can see your rhythms — maybe every six weeks there's a spike
- This is your **life's waveform**. Not data. Not charts. A landscape made of water.

## The Home Surface

When you open ostx, you see the underwater caustic surface — warm pearl light dancing on sand.

### Layout

- **Pool** sits center-low — the gravity well, your eye goes to it first. Fill level immediately visible.
- **Pebble** clusters sit around the pool — your current load, visible as small rounded forms
- **Droplets** drift in the upper area — recent thoughts, gently floating
- **Rings** orbit at the periphery — your people, at their current distances

Nothing is labeled. The shapes ARE the language.

### Navigation

No tab bar. No hamburger menu. Shapes are accessed through **directional gestures** from the surface:

- Tap a shape cluster to enter that shape's space
- Pull down to dive into time (Swell → Tide)
- Pull up to return to surface (now)
- Subtle visual hints show which direction leads where — soft glows at edges

### Caustics View

The background is a living GLSL shader — real-time caustic light patterns:

- Shifts with time of day (warm morning, cool evening)
- Activity balance affects pattern density
- Season of the year affects color temperature
- Intensity matches your Tide (high tide = active patterns, low tide = calm)

## Emotional Intelligence — The Quiet Engine

None of this is announced. It's felt.

### Presence Without Pressure

- The app never says "You haven't opened me in 3 days!" — when you return, caustics are a little slower, a little warmer. Welcome back.
- No streaks. No badges. No red dots. Instead: **Rhythms** — your natural patterns reflected without judgment.

### Emotional Breath

- After completing a heavy Pool item, the transition is slightly slower. The caustics settle. A microsecond of stillness. You did something hard. The app knows.
- After rapid-fire completion, the momentum matches — transitions quicken, the surface feels energized.

### Language

- Never "You failed to..." or "Overdue" or "Behind schedule"
- Instead: "This has been waiting" / "Still here when you're ready" / "Carried over"
- Completion language: "Settled" / "Released" / "Done" (user chooses their word)
- The app speaks in lowercase. Calm. No exclamation marks.

### Erosion

Tasks that sit too long don't turn red. They soften. The app gently asks: "Is this still yours to carry?" Maybe it's not a task anymore. Maybe it never was.

### Residue

Thoughts that keep recurring get subtly surfaced. The app notices your themes before you do.

### Moorings

People you haven't connected with in a while — their Ring widens. Not guilt. Just awareness.

### Lunar & Seasonal Awareness

- Subtle awareness of moon phases, solstices, seasonal shifts
- Not astrology — just acknowledgment that humans are animals and time is not just clocks
- On the solstice, the caustics shift. On a new moon, the Droplet space feels more open.

## Interaction Design

### Motion Principles — Bubble Physics

Every animation follows liquid/bubble physics:

1. **Surface tension** — Nothing snaps. Everything stretches, deforms, then settles. Drag a task and it elongates. Release and it wobbles back.

2. **Merge and split** — Navigating between shapes isn't a page transition. The current space *merges* into the next. One continuous liquid motion.

3. **Wobble, not bounce** — Nothing bounces (mechanical). Everything **wobbles** — organic oscillation of liquid finding equilibrium.

4. **Viscosity** — Interactions have a thick, honey-in-water quality. A slight drag behind your finger, like moving through warm water. The app follows at its own pace.

5. **Membrane** — Cards don't have borders. They have **membranes** — thin, pearlescent edges that catch light. Selected = membrane glows. Dismissed = membrane dissolves, content disperses like a popped bubble.

### Specific Animations

| Action | Animation |
|--------|-----------|
| Opening the app | A single bubble inflates from center, gently splits into the surface view |
| Entering a shape | The shape stretches toward center, envelops the screen — you're *inside* it |
| Adding an item | A small bubble rises from tap point, wobbles, settles into position |
| Completing a task | Membrane slowly thins, becomes transparent, pops with faint iridescent shimmer |
| Deleting | Bubble contracts and absorbs into nearest surface — returns to the liquid |
| Returning to surface | Current environment contracts back into its edge shape |
| Scrolling | Liquid lag — items near finger move first, others follow with viscous delay |

### Sound Design (Optional, off by default)

- Settling a task: soft, low tone — stone dropping into deep water
- Capturing a thought: brief, high shimmer
- No notification sounds. Ever.
- Ambient option: generative caustic audio — water refraction translated to sound

## Data Model

```
Shape: Pool | Pebble | Droplet | Ring

PoolEntry {
  id: string
  content: string
  sourceShape: Pebble | Droplet | Ring | null  // where it migrated from
  sourceId: string | null                       // original item id
  severity: rising | steady | receding          // emotional weight direction
  createdAt: timestamp
  resolvedAt: timestamp | null
  reflectionDropletId: string | null            // droplet left behind on resolution
}

PebbleEntry {
  id: string
  title: string
  weight: light | steady | heavy | crushing
  status: carrying | settled | released | eroded
  layers: [{ title: string, done: boolean }]    // subtasks as sediment
  createdAt: timestamp
  lastTouched: timestamp
  erodedAt: timestamp | null                    // when erosion was triggered
  poolMigratedAt: timestamp | null              // when it drifted to Pool
}

DropletEntry {
  id: string
  content: string
  captureType: typed | voice
  createdAt: timestamp
  surfacedCount: number                         // how often Residue brought it back
  clusterId: string | null                      // recurring theme cluster
  linkedPebbleId: string | null                 // if crystallized into a task
}

RingEntry {
  id: string
  personName: string
  orbitDistance: number                          // 1 (closest) to 5 (widest)
  weather: warm | cloudy | still | stormy
  notes: [{ content: string, date: timestamp }]
  gifts: string[]                               // ideas, things they like
  lastInteraction: timestamp
  createdAt: timestamp
}

WaveSnapshot {
  id: string
  date: string                                  // YYYY-MM-DD
  poolLevel: number                             // 0-1, how full the pool was
  pebbleCount: number
  dropletCount: number
  dominantWeight: light | steady | heavy        // overall day feel
  sunrise: timestamp                            // local dawn time
}
```

## Tech Stack

| Layer | Choice | Rationale |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Framework | Next.js 15 (App Router) | React, SSG-capable, PWA-ready |
| 3D/Shaders | Three.js + React Three Fiber | Declarative Three.js in React, shader integration |
| Shader language | GLSL (via drei shaderMaterial) | Full control over caustic/water effects |
| Motion | Framer Motion | Spring physics, layout animations, gestures |
| Light particles | Paper.js or raw Canvas 2D | Lightweight sparkle effects |
| Data | IndexedDB via Dexie.js | Local-first, structured, reactive queries |
| Styling | Tailwind CSS | Utility-first, easy to maintain pearl palette |
| PWA | next-pwa or Serwist | Installable on iOS, offline-capable |
| Typography | Averia Sans Libre (logo only) + DM Sans (everything else) | Handmade warmth for brand, clean geometry for UI |
| Deployment | Vercel | Zero-config for Next.js |

### Shader Strategy

| Effect | Technology | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Home caustics | Three.js + GLSL fragment shader | Real-time caustic patterns on a plane — the hero visual |
| Water surface | GLSL with Fresnel + noise | Shimmering surface above, subtle refraction |
| Bubble shapes | Three.js MeshPhysicalMaterial | Transmission, roughness, iridescence — liquid glass |
| Micro-interactions | CSS + Framer Motion | Wobble, settle, breathe |
| Scroll depth | CSS transforms + intersection observer | Parallax depth layers |
| Particle sparkles | Canvas 2D / Paper.js | Golden light flecks |
| Tide transitions | GLSL displacement shader | Ripple-morph between views |
| Text glow | CSS text-shadow | Warm light diffusing behind letterforms |

### Key Shader: Caustic Floor

The signature visual — light refracting through wavy water, projecting patterns on sand:

1. Layer 2-3 sine waves at different frequencies/directions (water surface)
2. Calculate refraction vectors through the surface
3. Where refracted rays converge = bright caustic lines
4. Animate wave phases slowly over time
5. Color: warm gold-white on pearl — never bright, always soft
6. Intensity varies with time of day

### Key Shader: Water Surface (Looking Up)

Subtle effect at top of home screen — underside of water:

1. Fresnel effect — edges more reflective
2. Perlin noise displacement for gentle wave motion
3. Blush-pink light bleeds through where surface is thinnest
4. Snell's window effect — circular bright area

## Data Philosophy

- **Everything is local-first.** Your inner life doesn't belong on a server.
- Optional encrypted sync via iCloud/CloudKit for multi-device
- **No analytics on your behavior.** Patterns learned locally, never phoned home.
- Export everything as plain text, always. Your thoughts are yours.
- No account creation. No login. You open the app and it's yours.
